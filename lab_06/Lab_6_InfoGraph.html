Text Mining with YouTube Data

An overview of the lab objectives, tasks, and connection to the final project.

Estimated Duration

2 hours

Objective

Extract and compare themes, patterns, and sentiments from captions and comments.

Background & Previous Skills

This lab builds upon techniques and skills developed in earlier labs.

Past Labs (1–5)

  • Collected and explored raw YouTube data (Lab 1).
  • Cleaned and preprocessed text for analysis (Lab 2).
  • Mined frequent patterns and co-occurrences (Lab 3).
  • Performed temporal/incremental analysis (Lab 4).
  • Clustered text data based on similarity (Lab 5).

This Lab (6)

  • Apply TF–IDF keyword extraction to both captions and comments.
  • Compare linguistic patterns between what’s said in the video and what’s discussed in the comments.
  • Extend analysis to n-grams, sentiment, and cross-video comparison.

Dataset Requirements

Requirement:

Each student or group must use a different video or channel so results are unique.

Choose one of the following datasets:

  • Your own cleaned dataset from previous labs (recommended).
  • A provided processed dataset ( cleaned_comments.csv , cleaned_captions.csv ).

Step-by-Step Lab Tasks

Detailed Tasks

A breakdown of the required steps to complete the lab.

Part A: Setup & Recall

  • Load cleaned comments and captions CSVs into pandas DataFrames.
  • Ensure there is a cleaned_tokens column.
  • Join tokens back into strings for text analysis.
  • Install scikit-learn and matplotlib-venn if not already available.

Part B: TF–IDF Keyword Extraction

Identify the most important keywords for both comments and captions.

  • Vectorize comments with TfidfVectorizer.
  • Extract top 15 keywords for comments and save to CSV.
  • Repeat for captions.

Part C: Keyword & Theme Comparison

Visually compare the keywords found in comments and captions.

Visualize the overlap and uniqueness of keywords using a Venn diagram.

Part D: N-gram Analysis

Analyze frequently co-occurring word pairs (bigrams).

  • Run TF–IDF for bigrams.
  • Extract top 10 bigrams for both comments and captions.
  • Compare overlap and uniqueness.

Part E: Sentiment Analysis

Measure the emotional tone of the comments and captions.

Visualize the sentiment distribution (positive/neutral/negative) with a bar chart.

Insights & Deliverables

Part F: Linking to Past Labs

Integrate previous lab techniques for a more comprehensive analysis.

  • Use Lab 3 techniques to find co-occurring keyword pairs from the top TF–IDF terms.
  • Use Lab 4’s temporal split (if your data spans time) to see how keyword prominence changes.
  • (Optional) Cluster TF–IDF vectors as in Lab 5 to group discussions thematically.

Part G: Insight Statements

Summarize your findings in 3–5 key statements.

  • Which themes dominate in captions but not in comments?
  • Are certain topics only raised by the audience?
  • How does sentiment differ between captions and comments?

Save as Lab6_Insights.txt.
